2026-04-28 — Plug clean recovers rear cylinder; rich-running condition identified

  • Type: diagnosis + temporary fix
  • Work done:
    • Pulled rear-cylinder LEFT plug (RR-L, easy access from left side, no tank prop). Plug was sooty and black, smelled of unburned petrol — not solvent. Wire-brushed, wiped dry, reseated.
    • Engine started on both cylinders again. Cleaning a single plug was enough because the XL650V has 2 plugs per cylinder — the cyl can fire on one working plug while the other is fouled.
    • Pulled front-cylinder RIGHT plug (FR-R) for comparison. Same sooty appearance — both cyls running rich, not just the rear. Cleaned and reinstalled.
    • Visible exhaust at idle — could be cold-weather water vapour or actual rich smoke; smell test pending on next start.
  • Findings:
    • Carb-cleaner-drowning theory was wrong — fuel smell ruled it out.
    • Both carbs running rich. Possible causes: PO set pilot screws too rich (he admitted tampering), float levels too high, clogged air filter, choke partly stuck on, vacuum slide diaphragm tear, or breached pilot screw o-ring (rich leak — see CBR250 precedent).
    • Rear pilot screw still stuck at ~¼ turn. Underlying mixture issue not fixed — plugs will re-foul.
  • Next: pull remaining 2 plugs (front-LH, rear-RH) for full picture; check air filter; free stuck pilot screw with penetrating oil; carb service with Keyster kit.
  • Shop/Self: self
