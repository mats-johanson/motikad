## Wild camping rules

### Estonia

- Wild camping allowed on public/state land (RMK forests)
- RMK maintains hundreds of free forest campsites with shelters, fire pits, firewood, outhouses
- Campfires only at designated fire sites
- Not allowed in nature reserves, private land without permission, or urban areas
- **App:** [RMK Loodusega Koos](apps.md#rmk-loodusega-koos--essential-for-estonia) (free, shows all sites)

### Latvia

- Wild camping permitted on public/state forest land (LVM forests)
- 300+ free recreation sites in LVM forests with tent areas and fire pits
- Campfires allowed, must be 2m from trees
- Not allowed in nature reserves/national parks (limited), private land without permission, urban areas
- **App:** LVM GEO + [Dodies.lv](apps.md#dodieslv--essential-for-latvia)

### Lithuania

- Wild camping tolerated on public land outside protected areas
- More restricted than Estonia/Latvia — fewer organized free sites
- National parks require designated camping areas
- **App:** [Park4Night](apps.md#park4night--essential-for-touring), [iOverlander](apps.md#ioverlander)

## Notable campsite areas

### Estonia

#### Lahemaa National Park area

Multiple RMK sites along the coast near Käsmu, Altja, and Palmse. Shelters, fire pits, forest settings within walking distance of the sea. Free. Pairs with the [Lahemaa Loop](routes.md#lahemaa-loop) day trip.

#### Saaremaa & Muhu islands

Several campgrounds on the islands. Combine with the [Saaremaa Circuit](routes.md#saaremaa-circuit). Good mix of paid sites near Kuressaare and free RMK spots in the interior.

#### Soomaa National Park edge

RMK campsites at the park boundary. Bog landscape, extremely quiet. Best in dry season (June–August).

#### Haanja Highlands / Rõuge

RMK sites scattered through the hills. Lake swimming nearby. Combine with [Haanja Highlands](routes.md#haanja-highlands) route.

#### Hiiumaa Island

Quiet campgrounds and RMK shelters. Minimal crowds even in peak season. Pair with [Hiiumaa Lighthouse Tour](routes.md#hiiumaa-lighthouse-tour).

### Latvia

#### Gauja National Park / Sigulda area

Campgrounds along the Gauja River valley. Combine with the [Gauja NP weekend trip](routes.md#gauja-national-park-latvia-300350-km-rt). Mix of paid sites (~10–15 EUR) and free LVM forest spots nearby.

#### Cape Kolka / Slītere NP

Remote Livonian coast. Simple campgrounds at the cape where two seas meet. Part of the [Baltic Coastal Run](routes.md#baltic-coastal-run-tallinn--klaipėda-34-days).

#### Vidzeme highlands

LVM forest sites throughout the highlands. Pair with the [Vidzeme "Dangerous Roads"](routes.md#vidzeme-dangerous-roads-latvia-23-days) route.

#### Riga — Two Wheels Hotel

[2w.lv](https://www.2w.lv/) — motorcycle-specific accommodation. 10 rooms in a 19th-century wooden building, 15 min walk from Old Town. Free guarded/roofed motorcycle parking, moto-adventure tours, motorcycle rental, laundry. Run by local riders; TET Latvia linesman Martins is connected here.

### Lithuania

#### Curonian Spit — Nida Camping

100 pitches between the Dune of Parnidis and the Baltic Sea. Showers, laundry, kitchen, bar, pool. Walking distance to beaches. The spot for the [Baltic Coastal Run](routes.md#baltic-coastal-run-tallinn--klaipėda-34-days) endpoint.

#### Aukštaitija National Park

Lake country between Molėtai and Ignalina. Campsites among the lakes. Part of the [Three Capitals](routes.md#three-capitals--lakes-57-days) route.

## Motocamping gear checklist

Packing light — two bikes, limited luggage capacity.

### Shelter

- [ ] Lightweight 1-person tent or bivvy
- [ ] Sleeping bag (comfort ~5°C for Baltic summer nights)
- [ ] Sleeping pad (inflatable, compact)

### Fire & cooking

- [ ] Compact stove (Jetboil/MSR Pocket Rocket)
- [ ] Fuel canister
- [ ] Lighter + backup
- [ ] Pot/mug combo
- [ ] Spork
- [ ] Water bottle

### Essentials

- [ ] Headlamp
- [ ] Phone charger (USB) + power bank
- [ ] First aid kit
- [ ] Dry bags for rain protection
- [ ] Rope/bungees for lashing gear
- [ ] Knife/multitool
- [ ] Trash bags (pack it out)

### Bike-specific

- [ ] Soft panniers or tail bag (SV650) / hard cases (Transalp)
- [ ] Tank bag with map window
- [ ] Rain gear (jacket + pants)
- [ ] Extra warm layer for evenings
- [ ] Lock + chain

## Tips

- **Season:** June–August is prime. May and September are rideable but expect cold nights (~5–10°C)
- **Mosquitoes:** Brutal near bogs and lakes June–July. Bring repellent and a tent with good mesh
- **Water:** RMK sites sometimes have hand pumps. Carry a filter or purification tabs as backup
- **Firewood:** RMK sites stock firewood. In Latvia, gather deadfall only
- **Ticks:** Vaccination for tick-borne encephalitis recommended — forests are the main riding terrain
- **Phone signal:** Surprisingly good across the Baltics, even rural areas. EU roaming works. Telia has best rural coverage
- **Border crossings:** No checks within Schengen (all three countries). Just ride through
- **Off-road legality (Estonia):** Need landowner permission for off-road riding. State forests (RMK) require contacting RMK directly. Stick to established trails
