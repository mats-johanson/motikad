## Route details

### Otepää–Puka Loop

Tartu → road 44 south to Otepää → Puka → Kanepi → back to Tartu

- **Road:** Paved, some rough patches (surface rated 2/5 in spots)
- **Bike:** SV650
- **Notes:** Rated 5/5 overall — hilliest terrain in Estonia ("Estonian Alps"). Route 175 (Puka–Otepää) is the 15 km highlight — biker.ee says "tow truck phone numbers used to be nailed to trees at the tight corners." Add the Otepää–Käärikü (7 km, steep descent, new asphalt) and Sihva–Nüpli–Otepää (7 km, blind curves with climbs) segments for extra mileage
- **Nearby additions:** Road 71 Otepää–Kanepi (19 km, recently paved, fast curves). Rõngu–Valga (fresh asphalt, smooth fast curves — on the way to Latvia)

### Postitee (Route 15)

Tartu → road 44 south toward Põlva → Route 15 (Postitee) between Põlva and Võru counties → Võru → return

- **Distance:** ~80 km round trip (the Postitee section itself is ~40 km)
- **Road:** Paved, original "3D shape — left, right, up, down"
- **Bike:** SV650 (or either)
- **Stops:** Estonian Road Museum (in an old post station along the route)
- **Notes:** An 18th-century stagecoach road preserved with its original terrain-following alignment. Called "Mecca for Estonia's drivers" by ERR. Fast flowing corners over rolling hills. Every Estonian source mentions this road. Combines naturally with the Otepää–Puka loop or the Haanja route

### Lake Peipus Loop

Tartu → road 43 northeast to Alatskivi → Kolkja → Alja → Mustvee → south shore → back to Tartu

- **Distance:** ~150 km loop
- **Road:** Paved
- **Bike:** SV650 (or either)
- **Stops:** Alatskivi castle, Old Believer villages along the Onion Route (Kolkja–Varnja, 7 km), onion-domed churches, Mustvee. Kolkja–Alja section rated 5/5 — "narrow winding road between trees — it scares you but you like it"
- **Notes:** Europe's 5th largest lake. ~30 min from Tartu to the shore. Eastern shoreline through Vasknarva and Kasepää has gaps where Emajõe Suursoo is impassable — adventure bikes needed for those shore roads

### Old Põlva Highway

Tartu → Puskaru → Ihamaru → Karilatsi → Võrekula → return

- **Distance:** ~50 km round trip (~24 km core section)
- **Road:** Paved
- **Bike:** SV650
- **Notes:** Close to Tartu. Climbs, descents, curves with blind sections between hills. Last 6–8 km especially good. Also nearby: Liiva–Prangli–Veski (~8 km, hilly curvy gravel — Transalp option)

### Tartu–Pärnu Scenic

Avoid E263. Take smaller roads through Viljandi county — road 92 via Põltsamaa or road 49 via Viljandi.

- **Road:** Paved, rated 4/5 surface, 4/5 corners
- **Bike:** SV650
- **Notes:** ~180 km one way. Low traffic, good corners. Pärnu beach town for a lunch stop, return via a different inland route to make a loop
- **Scenic Tallinn alternative:** If heading to Tallinn, take Piibe Road (Route 13) instead of E263 — branches past Jägala waterfall, includes a 3 km section of extra-wide straight tarmac (former airstrip reserve). Adds ~20 km but far more interesting

### Rakvere–Rannapungerja

Tartu → road 36 north to Jõgeva → road 39 to Rakvere → east through Ida-Viru county to Rannapungerja lighthouse → return via Mustvee/Peipus shore

- **Road:** Paved, rated 5/5
- **Bike:** SV650
- **Notes:** Full day trip from Tartu. Can combine with a Peipus shore stop on the return leg. Variation: Aseri–Kunda–Karepa–Vihula–Vergi–Võsu (~50 km of forest roads with curves along the northern coast)
- **Side trips from Jõgeva:** Laiuse–Kuremaa (road 5768, narrow tree-lined manor road), Luua–Pikkjärve (4–5 km, three hill jumps), Harjanurme–Puurmani (20 km gentle curves, castle at end)

### Haanja Highlands

Tartu → road 44 to Otepää → Võru → Haanja → Rõuge → Vastseliina → Setomaa → Põlva → Tartu

- **Road:** Mixed paved and gravel backroads
- **Bike:** Transalp
- **Stops:** Suur Munamägi (318m, highest point in Baltics), Rõuge lakes, Vastseliina castle ruins, Setomaa cultural region
- **Key segment:** Route 148 Haanja–Rõuge (9 km) — "excellent twisting tarmac through rolling hills." The Võru–Haanja–Rõuge–Nursi–Sõmerpalu–Võru loop has "over 90-degree curves, nearly hairpin turns." Vastse-Roosa to Orava lap is 85 km with 47 km of gravel
- **Notes:** ~80 km from Tartu to Võru, then the good stuff starts. The best adventure day trip from Tartu

### Setomaa & Saatse Boot

Tartu → Põlva → Võõpsu → Värska → Saatse → Obinitsa → Meremäe → Luhamaa → return via Põlva

- **Distance:** ~120 km loop
- **Road:** Mixed paved and gravel
- **Bike:** Transalp
- **Stops:** Seto Kulavüü (Village Belt) cultural trail, Obinitsa Seto museum
- **Notes:** The Saatse Boot (Saatse Saba) is a road that briefly passes through Russian territory without passport control — you cannot stop, only drive through, under video surveillance. A unique novelty. A gravel alternative exists to bypass the Russian segment. Combines well with Haanja for a full day

### Lake Peipus Backroads

Tartu → east to Luunja → gravel roads along the western shore → Alatskivi → Kolkja → Mustvee → return

- **Road:** Mixed paved and gravel
- **Bike:** Transalp
- **Stops:** Same as the paved loop but taking the smaller shore tracks. Fishing villages, old wooden architecture
- **Notes:** Shorter than the paved loop but slower on gravel

### Soomaa National Park

Tartu → road 49 to Viljandi (~80 km) → south to Soomaa → visitor center → return via Põltsamaa

- **Road:** Paved to park boundary, then dirt/gravel (30 km/h limit inside)
- **Bike:** Transalp
- **Notes:** ~100 km from Tartu to the park. Massive bog landscapes, least populated area in Estonia. Vehicles restricted to designated roads inside park. Best in dry season

### Otepää–Kanepi Gravel

Tartu → Otepää → forest tracks south toward Kanepi → Põlva → Tartu

- **Road:** Mix of paved and forest gravel roads through the hills
- **Bike:** Transalp
- **Notes:** The same "Estonian Alps" terrain as the paved loop but via the dirt tracks between the hilltops. Explore the backroads the SV650 can't reach

### Enduro near Tartu

Not a route but worth knowing — **Kukemetsa Quarry** is the closest enduro terrain to Tartu. Forest trails and overgrown gravel pits with sand, waves, technical sections. Regular training occurs here. Also: **Rõiu/Uniküla Forest** (~3–4 km past Vooremäed) has fairly hilly forest trails.

## Weekend trips (1–2 days)

### Lahemaa & North Coast (~400 km)

- **Day 1:** Tartu → road 36 north through Jõgeva → Lahemaa NP. Palmse Manor → Sagadi → Altja → Käsmu → overnight in the park or Loksa
- **Day 2:** Coast west to Tallinn or east to Rakvere → return to Tartu via inland roads
- **Road:** All paved, good surface
- **Bike:** SV650
- **Stops:** Palmse Manor, Sagadi Manor, Käsmu fishing village ("captain's village"), Altja tavern, Viru Bog boardwalk. Leesi–Loksa (28 km, "entrance to paradise" — continuous curves, top-rated on biker.ee). Vihula–Võsu (21 km, dense curves, new asphalt)
- **Extension east:** Continue along the Klint Road to Ontika–Valaste–Toila (50m coastal cliffs, Valaste waterfall at 32m — highest in Estonia, sharp curves). Extends into Alutaguse NP (Estonia's wildest — bears, inland sand dunes, bogs)

### West Estonia & Islands (~500 km)

- **Day 1:** Tartu → Viljandi → Pärnu → Virtsu → ferry to Muhu → causeway to Saaremaa → Kuressaare. Overnight Kuressaare
- **Day 2:** Saaremaa circuit (Kaali crater, Angla windmills, Sõrve Peninsula, Orissaare–Kalmu coastal road) → ferry back → return via Pärnu or Viljandi
- **Road:** Mostly paved, short gravel sections on islands
- **Bike:** Either
- **Notes:** Orissaare–Kalmu (88 km, rated 4.5/5) — "best road profile in Estonia". Jämaja rated 5/5 (8 km, leads to Panga cliff). Orissaare–Leisi Ranna Road (new asphalt, coastal). Book ferry Virtsu–Muhu in advance during summer
- **Add-on:** Vormsi Island (55 km tour, ~350 residents, Swedish heritage) — ferry from Haapsalu. Or Noarootsi Peninsula / Matsalu NP for coastal bogs and birdwatching

### Hiiumaa Lighthouses (~450 km)

- **Day 1:** Tartu → Pärnu (~180 km) → Rohuküla → ferry to Heltermaa (~1.5h) → Kõpu Lighthouse → overnight Kärdla
- **Day 2:** Ristna Lighthouse → Tahkuna Lighthouse → Kassari island → ferry back → return to Tartu
- **Road:** Mixed paved and gravel
- **Bike:** Transalp preferred, SV650 manageable on main roads
- **Stops:** Kõpu Lighthouse (16th century, one of the oldest in the world), Ristna, Tahkuna, Kassari (Estonia's 5th largest island — biker.ee highlight), military museum, empty beaches. Road 84 Kõpu–Luidja rated 4/5

### Gauja National Park (Latvia, ~350 km RT)

- **Day 1:** Tartu → Valga (border, ~80 km) → Valmiera → Sigulda → Gauja NP. Sigulda–Līgatne trail 30 km along river. Overnight Sigulda
- **Day 2:** Turaida Castle → Cēsis medieval town → return via Valga to Tartu
- **Road:** Paved main roads, gravel park roads
- **Bike:** Either
- **Stops:** Sigulda ("Switzerland of Latvia"), Turaida Castle, Gutman's Cave (largest in Baltics), Cēsis castle ruins
- **Notes:** Closer from Tartu than from Tallinn — Valga border is only 80 km south. Nearby: Vecpiebalga GP (18 km, rated 5/5) — "fast and winding, untypical for Latvia, reminds of Alpine b-roads." Excellent asphalt, tight corners. Best twisty road in Latvia

### Tartu–Riga via Coast (~500 km loop)

- **Day 1:** Tartu → Pärnu (scenic inland roads) → coastal road south → Ikla–Häädemeeste rannatee (30 km) → Jūrmala → Riga
- **Day 2:** Riga old town morning → return via Gauja NP (Cēsis/Valmiera) → Valga → Tartu
- **Road:** Paved
- **Bike:** SV650
- **Notes:** Down via coast, back via inland — see everything without backtracking
- **Riga stay:** Two Wheels Hotel (2w.lv) — motorcycle-specific, 10 rooms, free guarded/roofed moto parking, 15 min from Old Town. Run by local riders, TET Latvia linesman connected here

### Tallinn Coast Day (~400 km RT)

- **Day 1:** Tartu → Tallinn via Piibe Road/Route 13 (~205 km scenic) → Paldiski → Keila-Joa waterfall → Tallinn coast → overnight
- **Day 2:** Tallinn → scenic return to Tartu via Lahemaa or Paide
- **Road:** Paved
- **Bike:** SV650
- **Stops:** Keila waterfall, Paldiski Soviet submarine base ruins, Tallinn old town, Kaberneeme–Valkla (short coastal curves)

## Multi-day tours (3–7 days)

### Baltic Coastal Run: Tartu → Klaipėda (3–4 days)

~900 km, paved. SV650.

- **Day 1:** Tartu → Pärnu (scenic inland, ~180 km) → coastal road south → Ikla border → Riga (~350 km total)
- **Day 2:** Riga → Jūrmala → Cape Kolka/Slītere NP → overnight Kolka or Ventspils (~160 km). Side trip: Kuldīga ("Venice of Latvia") via Abava Valley (Kuldīga–Tukums 99 km, rated 5/5 — "Switzerland of Courland")
- **Day 3:** Ventspils → coastal south through Liepāja → Klaipėda (~250 km)
- **Day 4:** Curonian Spit — ferry from New Ferry Terminal (Nemuno St. 8), first-come-first-served. ~10 EUR moto park entry. Single road 50 km to Nida, **50 km/h strictly enforced** (cameras + police). One gas station (in Nida). Go on a weekday — weekends are packed
- **Return:** DFDS ferry Klaipėda → Karlshamn (Sweden) or ride back via inland (~700 km, 1–2 days)

### Latvia Deep Dive (3–4 days)

~800 km from Tartu. Paved. SV650.

- **Day 1:** Tartu → Valga → Cēsis → Vecpiebalga GP (18 km twisties) → Sigulda → overnight Riga
- **Day 2:** Riga → Kuldīga via Abava Valley (99 km, rated 5/5) → overnight Kuldīga or Ventspils
- **Day 3:** Cape Kolka loop (66 km through Slītere NP) → Daugava River Road (121 km, riverside cruise) → return via Aizkraukle–Jēkabpils (41 km, rated 5/5) → Valga → Tartu
- **Stops:** Vecpiebalga (Alpine-style twisties), Kuldīga (Ventas Rumba — Europe's widest waterfall, UNESCO tentative), Cape Kolka (where two seas meet)

### Three Capitals (5–7 days)

~1300 km from Tartu, paved with optional off-road. Either bike.

Tartu → Otepää → Valga → Sigulda/Gauja NP → Riga → Rundāle Palace → Hill of Crosses → Aukštaitija NP (Molėtai–Ignalina lakes road, 65 km) → Vilnius → Trakai Castle → Curonian Spit → return via coast or ferry

**Lithuania highlights:** Vilnius–Anykščiai–Kupišķis–Zarasai loop (417 km, rated 5/5 — northeast lake district). Dubingiai (21 km, rated 5/5). Biržai–Anykščiai–Taujenai (146 km, rated 4.5/5 — 9,000+ sinkholes in Biržai karst region). Dzūkija NP (697 km², 91% forest, 12 bike routes, good Transalp territory)

**Notes:** Starting from Tartu saves ~200 km vs Tallinn start

### TET Baltic Adventure (5–7 days)

~3350 km total (pick sections). Gravel/forest roads. **Transalp only.**

- **Estonia:** 1054 km, 2 tracks (western through islands, eastern along Latvian border). ~4 days. Features "Randel's Triple" — three steep climbs with swampy bogs between. Eastern track is closer to Tartu. GPX from transeurotrail.org/estonia/
- **Latvia:** 1802 km, 3 sections, 2–4 days each. Enter via Gaujiena from Valga. Centre track has heavy sand, mud, river crossings. Stay at Two Wheels Hotel in Riga (connected to TET linesman Martins)
- **Lithuania:** 495 km, 3 days. Gets harder north to south. **Deep sand sections** — one notorious 100m sandy hill near Kažlų Rūda defeated riders on 200+ kg bikes. The Transalp is borderline here; practice loaded sand riding before attempting. Knobbly tires mandatory
- **Alternative:** Outduro Latvia Border Route (2,586 km) — designed for larger adventure bikes. Mostly gravel with some sand. GPX available from outduro.com
- **Finland TET:** ~8,000 km total network. Section 01 (Helsinki to Norway) has the most variety. Gravel starts ~1h outside Helsinki. The Finnish Lake District section rides through 180,000+ bodies of water. Wild camping legal. GPX from transeurotrail.org/finland/

### Nordic Loop via Finland (5–7 days)

~1700–2200 km, paved. SV650.

- **Day 1:** Tartu → Tallinn (~185 km) → ferry to Helsinki (2h 15m) → Porvoo (50 km coastal)
- **Day 2:** Helsinki → King's Road (Kuninkaantie) to Turku (~400 km scenic, medieval mail road from 14th century — "embraces every hill and valley"). Or shorter: Helsinki → Turku via highway (~170 km)
- **Day 3:** Turku Archipelago Ring Road (250 km loop, 20,000+ islands, 5 ferry crossings, most free)
- **Day 4:** Turku → ferry to Stockholm (overnight, ~10.5h) or ferry back to Tallinn
- **Archipelago tips:** Go **counter-clockwise** in peak summer to avoid ferry queues. Full ferry service May–August only (September: first 3 weekends only). Iniö–Kustavi ferry is infrequent — check finnferries.fi. Missing the last ferry strands you overnight. Book accommodation in Nagu or Korpo in advance late June–early August. Best food: L'Escale in Nagu, Sattmark Cafe (1750s cottage)
- **Notes:** Add ~185 km each way for the Tartu–Tallinn leg. Use FMI Weather app for Finland forecasts

### Finnish Lakeland / Saimaa (5–7 days)

~2000 km, paved. SV650.

The biggest gap in the original route list. Finland's interior is where the real riding is — Route 62 is rated one of the 5 most beautiful roads in the Nordics.

- **Day 1:** Tartu → Tallinn → ferry to Helsinki → Porvoo (~50 km coastal)
- **Day 2:** Helsinki → Lahti (100 km) → **Pulkkilanharju Ridge** (8+ km ice-age ridge with water on both sides — "road cuts right through a lake") → Mikkeli
- **Day 3:** **Route 62: Mikkeli → Puumala → Ruokolahti** — selected as one of 5 most beautiful Nordic routes. Hilly, rocky, following Lake Saimaa's shores. Stops: Anttola, Ollinmäki winery, Pistohiekka beaches
- **Day 4:** Road 4793 (twisty) to **Punkaharju Ridge** — narrow ridge dividing two lakes, water metres from the road on both sides. Continue to Savonlinna (medieval Olavinlinna castle, summer opera festival)
- **Day 5:** Return to Helsinki via Route 14/5 or continue to Turku for Archipelago Ring Road
- **Alternative:** Green Gold Culture Road (250 km, Mäntyharju to Parikkala) — forest-themed route through the lake district

**Notes:** Can combine with the Archipelago leg: ferry to Helsinki → Lakeland (3 days) → back to Helsinki → Turku → Archipelago (2 days). Mototour of Nations 2026 is in Imatra (Aug 11–14) — right in the Saimaa region

### Norway via Stockholm (7–10 days)

~3000–3500 km total, paved. SV650.

The big one. Trollstigen, Atlantic Road, and Norwegian Scenic Routes. Tight but doable in a week if you skip the Archipelago.

- **Day 1:** Tartu → Tallinn → ferry to Helsinki (or Paldiski → Kapellskär direct to Sweden)
- **Day 2:** Helsinki → ferry to Stockholm (overnight) or ride through Sweden from Kapellskär
- **Day 3:** Stockholm → northwest into Norway → **Rondane** (Scenic Route, 75 km, 700–1060m elevation)
- **Day 4:** → **Sognefjellet** (108 km, highest mountain pass in northern Europe, 1,434m) or **Valdresflye** (49 km, 890–1,389m, above treeline)
- **Day 5:** → **Geiranger–Trollstigen** (104 km, 11 hairpin bends, 850m elevation gain). Trollstigen open mid-May to October
- **Day 6:** → **Atlantic Road / Atlanterhavsvegen** (36 km, iconic bridges across the open sea)
- **Day 7:** → Trondheim or begin return east via E14
- **Day 8–9:** Return to Stockholm → ferry → Tallinn → Tartu
- **Norwegian Scenic Routes:** 18 designated routes, all free. Full list at nasjonaleturistveger.no. Also nearby: Aurlandsfjellet ("snow road between fjords," 47 km, seasonal), Gamle Strynefjellsvegen (27 km, historic mountain passage)
- **Practical:** Norway is expensive (fuel, food, tolls). Strict speed enforcement. Weather changes fast — always have Plan B. Wild camping legal (150–200m from houses). FerryPay service recommended for road ferries. Best months: July–August

### Vidzeme "Dangerous Roads" (Latvia, 2–3 days)

~400 km from Tartu. Almost entirely gravel. **Transalp.**

Tartu → Valga (~80 km) → Valmiera → Cēsis → Route P79 toward Ērgļi → Koknese → loop back via Cēsis → Valga → Tartu

Listed on dangerousroads.org — constant sharp bends, up-down through Vidzeme highlands. Closer from Tartu via Valga than from Riga. Add: Suntaži–Ērgļi (32 km, rated 4.5/5 — impressive bridges on former railway), Nītaure–Zaube (9 km, rated 5/5)

## Speed cameras

Watch out — the Baltics have aggressive enforcement.

### Estonia

- 67 fixed camera cabins on state roads
- **Route 2 (Tallinn–Tartu–Võru–Luhamaa): 12 cabins, km 89–206** — your main road south
- Tallinn–Pärnu–Ikla: 10 cabins, km 92–143
- Tallinn–Narva: 15 cabins, km 93–202
- AI-equipped patrol cars and average speed cameras coming (2026–2035 plan)
- Camera locations: tarktee.ee

### Latvia

- Extensive fixed camera network (8.5 million EUR in fines in 2025)
- **Average speed cameras active** on A1 (km 13–20), A2 (km 54–62), A6 (km 102–160). 17 new sections coming 2026
- Fines escalate fast: 21–30 over = 40–80 EUR; 51+ over = 240–680 EUR; motorcycle 61–70 over = 720–960 EUR + 9-month ban
- Camera map: csdd.lv/en/speed-cameras

### Lithuania

- 70 fixed cameras on state roads (both directions)
- 25 average-speed sections covering 110 km
- Mobile speed traps at hotspots, not always signed
- Curonian Spit: 50 km/h strictly enforced throughout

## Practical notes

- **Season:** May–September. April/October possible but cold nights. Best: June–August
- **Speed limits:** Urban 50 (sometimes 30), rural 90, expressway 110. Latvia gravel: 80
- **Lights:** Always on (all three countries)
- **Alcohol:** Zero tolerance in Estonia, near-zero in Latvia/Lithuania
- **Fuel:** Plentiful near cities. Plan ahead in national parks and eastern regions. Compare prices: fuelest.ee (Estonia)
- **Emergency:** 112 everywhere
- **Currency:** Euro in all three countries
- **EU roaming:** Works throughout. Telia has best rural coverage in Estonia
- **Ticks:** Vaccination for tick-borne encephalitis recommended if riding through forests
- **Off-road legality (Estonia):** Need landowner permission. State forests (RMK) require contacting RMK directly. Stick to established trails — road register (Teeregister) can verify legal status
- **Tartu to key distances:** Valga/Latvia border 80 km, Pärnu 180 km, Tallinn 185 km, Virtsu ferry 220 km
