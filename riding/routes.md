## Route details

### Lahemaa Loop

Tallinn → E20 east ~55 km → north toward Loksa/Palmse → Palmse Manor → Sagadi → Altja → Käsmu → Vihula → E20 → Tallinn

- **Road:** All paved, good surface
- **Bike:** SV650
- **Stops:** Palmse Manor, Sagadi Manor, Käsmu fishing village ("captain's village"), Altja tavern, Viru Bog boardwalk

### Tallinn–Pärnu Scenic

Avoid E67/Via Baltica. Take smaller inland roads through Rapla county.

- **Road:** Paved, rated 4/5 surface, 4/5 corners
- **Bike:** SV650
- **Notes:** Low traffic, good corners. "On a sunny day this route is a must"

### Otepää–Puka Loop

Tartu → road 44 south to Otepää → Puka → Kanepi → back to Tartu

- **Road:** Paved, some rough patches (surface rated 2/5 in spots)
- **Bike:** SV650
- **Notes:** Rated 5/5 overall — hilliest terrain in Estonia ("Estonian Alps"). Rolling forests, dense countryside

### Saaremaa Circuit

Ferry Virtsu → Muhu → causeway to Saaremaa → Kuressaare → Kaali crater → Angla windmills → Sõrve Peninsula → Orissaare–Kalmu coastal road

- **Road:** Mostly paved, short gravel sections
- **Bike:** Either
- **Stops:** Kaali meteorite crater, Kuressaare castle, Angla windmills, Sõrve lighthouse
- **Notes:** Orissaare–Kalmu (88 km, rated 4.5/5) — "best road profile in Estonia". Sõrve Peninsula (63 km, rated 4/5)

### Paldiski–Keila-Joa

Short afternoon ride from Tallinn along the coast.

- **Road:** Paved
- **Bike:** SV650
- **Stops:** Keila waterfall, Paldiski Soviet submarine base ruins

### Rakvere–Rannapungerja

Medieval castle to lighthouse through quiet Ida-Viru county.

- **Road:** Paved, rated 5/5
- **Bike:** SV650

### Haanja Highlands

Võru → Haanja → Rõuge → Vastseliina → Setomaa → Põlva → Tartu

- **Road:** Mixed paved and gravel backroads
- **Bike:** Transalp
- **Stops:** Suur Munamägi (318m, highest point in Baltics), Rõuge lakes, Setomaa cultural region

### Hiiumaa Lighthouse Tour

Ferry from Rohuküla (~1.5h) → Heltermaa → Kõpu Lighthouse → Ristna Lighthouse → Tahkuna Lighthouse → loop

- **Road:** Mixed paved and gravel
- **Bike:** Transalp preferred (gravel sections), SV650 manageable on main roads
- **Stops:** Kõpu Lighthouse (16th century, one of the oldest in the world), military museum, empty beaches. Road 84 Kõpu–Luidja rated 4/5

### Soomaa National Park

Pärnu → road toward Tori → Soomaa visitor center

- **Road:** Paved to park boundary, then dirt/gravel (30 km/h limit inside)
- **Bike:** Transalp
- **Notes:** Massive bog landscapes. Vehicles restricted to designated roads inside park

### Lake Peipus Shore

Kolkja–Alja section (8 km, rated 5/5). Combine with surrounding roads.

- **Road:** Mixed
- **Bike:** Either
- **Stops:** Old Believer villages, onion-domed churches along Europe's 5th largest lake

## Weekend trips (1–2 days)

### West Estonia & Islands Loop (~450–500 km)

- **Day 1:** Tallinn → Haapsalu (90 km coastal) → Matsalu NP → ferry to Hiiumaa → lighthouse tour → overnight Kärdla
- **Day 2:** Ferry to Saaremaa → island circuit → ferry back → Pärnu → Tallinn
- **Road:** Mostly paved, gravel on islands
- **Bike:** Either

### South Estonia Highlands (~400 km)

- **Day 1:** Tallinn → Tartu (avoid E263) → Otepää → Haanja → overnight Võru/Rõuge
- **Day 2:** Setomaa → Põlva → Lake Peipus western shore → Tartu → Tallinn
- **Road:** Mixed
- **Bike:** Transalp ideal, SV650 stays on paved

### Tallinn–Riga Coastal Run (~450 km scenic)

- **Day 1:** Tallinn → scenic to Pärnu → coastal road south → Ikla–Häädemeeste rannatee (30 km) → Jūrmala → Riga
- **Day 2:** Return via Gauja NP inland (Valmiera/Cēsis)
- **Road:** Paved
- **Bike:** SV650

### Gauja National Park (Latvia, ~300–350 km RT)

- **Day 1:** Tallinn → Valga (border) → Valmiera → Sigulda → Gauja NP. Sigulda–Līgatne trail 30 km along river
- **Day 2:** Turaida Castle → Cēsis → return via Tartu
- **Road:** Paved main roads, gravel park roads
- **Bike:** Either
- **Stops:** Sigulda ("Switzerland of Latvia"), Turaida Castle, Gutman's Cave, Cēsis castle

## Multi-day tours (3–7 days)

### Baltic Coastal Run: Tallinn → Klaipėda (3–4 days)

~900–1000 km, paved. SV650.

- **Day 1:** Tallinn → Pärnu (scenic) → Ikla border → Riga (~350 km)
- **Day 2:** Riga → Jūrmala → Cape Kolka/Slītere NP → overnight Kolka or Ventspils (~160 km)
- **Day 3:** Ventspils → coastal south through Liepāja → Klaipėda (~250 km)
- **Day 4:** Curonian Spit — ferry to Smiltynė, ride south to Nida (50 km), sand dunes, Hill of Witches
- **Return:** DFDS ferry Klaipėda → Karlshamn (Sweden) or ride back

### Three Capitals + Lakes (5–7 days)

~1500 km, paved with optional off-road. Either bike.

Tallinn → Tartu → Otepää → Sigulda/Gauja → Riga → Rundāle Palace → Hill of Crosses → Aukštaitija NP (Molėtai–Ignalina lakes road, 65 km) → Vilnius → Trakai Castle → Curonian Spit → return or ferry

### TET Baltic Adventure (5–7 days)

~3350 km total (pick sections). Gravel/forest roads. **Transalp only.**

- **Estonia:** 1054 km, 2 tracks (western through islands, eastern along Latvian border). ~4 days. Features "Randel's Triple" — three steep climbs with swampy bogs between
- **Latvia:** 1802 km, 3 sections, 2–4 days each. Sandy sections near Riga
- **Lithuania:** 495 km, 3 days. Gets harder north to south. Knobbly tires mandatory

### Nordic Loop via Finland (5–7 days)

~1500–2000 km, paved. SV650.

- **Day 1:** Tallinn → ferry to Helsinki (2h15m) → Porvoo (50 km, scenic coastal road)
- **Day 2–3:** Helsinki → Turku (~170 km) → Archipelago Ring Road (250 km loop, 20,000+ islands, 5 ferry crossings, most free)
- **Day 4:** Turku → ferry to Stockholm (overnight) or back to Tallinn
- **Notes:** Turku Archipelago Ring Road is internationally recognized as a top motorcycle route

### Vidzeme "Dangerous Roads" (Latvia, 2–3 days)

~300–400 km from Riga. Almost entirely gravel. **Transalp.**

Riga → Cēsis → Route P79 toward Ērgļi → Koknese → loop back. Listed on dangerousroads.org — constant sharp bends, up-down through Vidzeme highlands.

## Practical notes

- **Season:** May–September. April/October possible but cold nights
- **Speed limits:** Urban 50 (sometimes 30), rural 90, expressway 110
- **Lights:** Always on (all three countries)
- **Alcohol:** Zero tolerance in Estonia, near-zero in Latvia/Lithuania
- **Fuel:** Plentiful near cities. Plan ahead in national parks and eastern regions
- **Emergency:** 112 everywhere
- **Currency:** Euro in all three countries
- **EU roaming:** Works throughout
