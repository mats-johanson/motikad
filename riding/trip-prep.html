Camping

Wild camping rules

Estonia
  • Wild camping allowed on public/state land (RMK forests)
  • RMK maintains hundreds of free forest campsites with shelters, fire pits, firewood, outhouses
  • Campfires only at designated fire sites
  • Not allowed in nature reserves, private land without permission, or urban areas
  • App: RMK Loodusega Koos (free, shows all sites)
Latvia
  • Wild camping permitted on public/state forest land (LVM forests)
  • 300+ free recreation sites in LVM forests with tent areas and fire pits
  • Campfires allowed, must be 2m from trees
  • Not allowed in nature reserves/national parks (limited), private land without permission, urban areas
  • App: LVM GEO + Dodies.lv
Lithuania
  • Wild camping tolerated on public land outside protected areas
  • More restricted than Estonia/Latvia — fewer organized free sites
  • National parks require designated camping areas
  • App: Park4Night, iOverlander

Notable campsite areas

Estonia

Lahemaa National Park area

Multiple RMK sites along the coast near Käsmu, Altja, and Palmse. Shelters, fire pits, forest settings within walking distance of the sea. Free. Pairs with the Lahemaa Loop day trip.

Saaremaa & Muhu islands

Several campgrounds on the islands. Combine with the Saaremaa Circuit. Good mix of paid sites near Kuressaare and free RMK spots in the interior.

Soomaa National Park edge

RMK campsites at the park boundary. Bog landscape, extremely quiet. Best in dry season (June–August).

Haanja Highlands / Rõuge

RMK sites scattered through the hills. Lake swimming nearby. Combine with Haanja Highlands route.

Hiiumaa Island

Quiet campgrounds and RMK shelters. Minimal crowds even in peak season. Pair with Hiiumaa Lighthouse Tour.

Latvia

Gauja National Park / Sigulda area

Campgrounds along the Gauja River valley. Combine with the Gauja NP weekend trip. Mix of paid sites (~10–15 EUR) and free LVM forest spots nearby.

Cape Kolka / Slītere NP

Remote Livonian coast. Simple campgrounds at the cape where two seas meet. Part of the Baltic Coastal Run.

Vidzeme highlands

LVM forest sites throughout the highlands. Pair with the Vidzeme "Dangerous Roads" route.

Riga — Two Wheels Hotel

2w.lv — motorcycle-specific accommodation. 10 rooms in a 19th-century wooden building, 15 min walk from Old Town. Free guarded/roofed motorcycle parking, moto-adventure tours, motorcycle rental, laundry. Run by local riders; TET Latvia linesman Martins is connected here.

Lithuania

Curonian Spit — Nida Camping

100 pitches between the Dune of Parnidis and the Baltic Sea. Showers, laundry, kitchen, bar, pool. Walking distance to beaches. The spot for the Baltic Coastal Run endpoint.

Aukštaitija National Park

Lake country between Molėtai and Ignalina. Campsites among the lakes. Part of the Three Capitals route.

  • Spark plug socket (18 mm deep) + gap tool

  • Tire pressure gauge

  • 12V mini compressor or frame pump

  • Spare fuses (matching your fuse box)
  • Electrical tape + wire connectors
  • Chain lube
  • Small bottle of engine oil (~200 ml)
  • Zip ties, duct tape, bailing wire

Tips

  • Season: June–August is prime. May and September are rideable but expect cold nights (~5–10°C)
  • Mosquitoes: Brutal near bogs and lakes June–July. Bring repellent and a tent with good mesh
  • Water: RMK sites sometimes have hand pumps. Carry a filter or purification tabs as backup
  • Firewood: RMK sites stock firewood. In Latvia, gather deadfall only
  • Ticks: Vaccination for tick-borne encephalitis recommended — forests are the main riding terrain
  • Phone signal: Surprisingly good across the Baltics, even rural areas. EU roaming works. Telia has best rural coverage
  • Border crossings: No checks within Schengen (all three countries). Just ride through
  • Off-road legality (Estonia): Need landowner permission for off-road riding. State forests (RMK) require contacting RMK directly. Stick to established trails

Navigation & route planning

Kurviger — primary

iOS, Android, Web. German-made, OSM-based.

  • Curviness slider — dial in exactly how twisty you want the route
  • Avoids cities and highways by default
  • Round-trip generator
  • Full GPX import/export
  • Offline maps (Tourer tier)
  • Direct transfer to Garmin GPS
  • Per-section road-type customization (set one leg "fast and curvy," another "twisty") — unique and useful for mixed terrain

Pricing: Free (fully functional). Tourer ~15 EUR/year, Tourer+ ~30 EUR/year

Baltic tip: Use "avoid motorways" mode, not "extra twisty." The Baltics are flat — the algorithm can't create curves that don't exist, and forcing it produces bad routing through farmyards and dead-ends. On "super-twisty" setting, always check the route before riding (forum riders warn of ridiculous detours)

Limitation: Waypoint view doesn't show distance/time between stops — plan fuel stops separately in rural areas

68 degrees — free alternative

iOS, Android, Web (app68.eu).

  • Free motorcycle GPS covering all of Europe up to Turkey
  • Unlimited waypoints and route length (no paywall)
  • Built-in tourist guide with hidden gems, accommodations with secure parking, gas stations
  • No registration required

Pricing: Free

Baltic fit: Worth evaluating as a Kurviger supplement for trip discovery. "Planned route is followed to the letter, right down to the bend"

Calimoto

iOS, Android. 3M+ users.

  • Auto-generates routes by curviness, duration, direction
  • Voice-guided turn-by-turn with caution alerts
  • GPX import, offline maps (premium)

Pricing: ~60 USD/year or ~8.50 USD/week. No free tier beyond 14-day trial

Baltic fit: Users report it "frequently says it cannot find roads they know exist" and "directs riders along closed roads, adding 45 minutes." Costs 4x Kurviger. Not recommended as primary

Scenic

iOS only.

  • Best-rated offline navigation (4.7 stars, 30M+ riding hours). Once map downloaded, fully offline
  • Round-trip generation, glove-friendly interface, Apple CarPlay support

Pricing: Lifetime ~110 EUR. Offline maps additional per region

Baltic fit: Good but iOS-only and expensive

MyRoute-app

iOS, Android, Web (desktop-first planner).

  • Street View integration for route preview
  • Scenic roads highlighted in green
  • Gas stop planning built in
  • Many export formats (GPX, ITN, KML)

Pricing: Free (basic). Gold ~5 EUR/month or 89 EUR lifetime

Baltic fit: Good for desktop pre-planning, push to phone

Detecht — crash detection only

iOS, Android.

  • Automatic crash detection and emergency alerts — real safety feature for solo riding in remote areas
  • Speed cameras work in the Baltics

Pricing: Free (basic). Premium varies

Warning: Do not use as primary navigation — users report it "constantly bugs riders trying to make them turn at every street only to circle back." Use only for crash detection

Maps & forest roads

OsmAnd — offline maps (critical)

iOS, Android.

  • Full offline maps from OpenStreetMap
  • Download entire countries — motorcycle navigation profile, voice guidance fully offline

Pricing: Free (limited downloads on iOS). OsmAnd+ ~30 EUR one-time

Must-do: Download Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania maps before departure

Baltic caveat: OSM data for Estonia covers all cities and highways, but ~31% of total roads are missing or outdated (avg data age: 1 year 8 months). Gravel roads may render identically to paved — do not trust surface rendering blindly. Cross-reference with Estonian National Geoportal (Maa-amet) or LVM GEO for ground truth

LVM GEO — Latvia forest roads

iOS, Android. Latvia's State Forests app.

  • Extensive map selection with all forest roads in state forests
  • Offline map downloads
  • Shows wild camping grounds in LVM forests

Pricing: Free

Baltic fit: Critical for gravel/adventure riding in Latvia. The Latvian equivalent of OsmAnd but with authoritative forest road data

Organic Maps

iOS, Android.

  • Open-source Maps.me fork — cleaner, no ads, no tracking
  • Hiking trails Google Maps misses
  • Fully offline, free
Google Maps
  • No motorcycle mode in Europe. Only available in select Asian markets. Treats motorcycles as cars — won't avoid highways or suggest moto-friendly routes
  • Still useful for POI search and city navigation

Camping & overnight

RMK Loodusega Koos — essential for Estonia

iOS, Android. Official app from Estonian State Forest Management Centre.

  • All free RMK campsites, shelters, fire pits, water sources
  • Hundreds of maintained sites with firewood, outhouses, shelters
  • Search by type (campsite, shelter, fire pit), region, and location

Pricing: Free. The campsites are free

Offline limitation: GPS positioning works without data, but the base map requires connectivity. Workaround: use OsmAnd for the base map and RMK app for campsite details when you have signal. Or check coordinates online before departure and save as waypoints

Dodies.lv — essential for Latvia

iOS, Android.

  • Nature trails, birdwatching towers, campsites, tent spots in Latvia, Estonia, Lithuania
  • Each location personally verified by developers

Pricing: Free

Park4Night — essential for touring

iOS, Android, Web.

  • 100,000+ overnight spots across Europe
  • Wild camping areas, free parking, scenic spots, paid campsites
  • Community-driven with photos and reviews

Pricing: Free (ads). Park4Night+ ~10 EUR/year (offline, no ads, satellite view)

iOverlander

iOS, Android, Web.

  • Global database: campsites, wild camping, water, mechanics, border crossings

Pricing: Free

Baltic fit: Sparser than Park4Night in Europe, useful as secondary source

Weather

Yr.no — primary (Baltics)

iOS, Android, Web. Norwegian Meteorological Institute.

  • Gold standard for Northern European weather. Estonian/Baltic locals prefer Yr.no
  • Hourly precipitation forecasts

Pricing: Free, no ads

FMI Weather — primary (Finland)

iOS, Android. Finnish Meteorological Institute.

  • Rain/lightning radar animations, weather warnings for Finland
  • 10-day forecasts

Pricing: Free

Notes: Finnish riders use this, not Yr.no. Essential for the Nordic Loop

Windy.com — radar & decisions

iOS, Android, Web.

  • Animated weather visualization, 6 forecast models side by side (ECMWF, GFS, ICON)
  • Rain radar, wind maps

Pricing: Free. Premium ~20 EUR/year

Best use: "Should I wait 2 hours or reroute?" decisions — better than Yr.no's single model

Drive Weather — route-specific

iOS, Android.

  • Shows weather along your exact route at estimated arrival time
  • Motorcycle wind chill calculation factoring rider direction
  • Icy pavement indicator

Pricing: Free (2-day). Pro ~10 USD/year (7-day, wind data)

Fuel & traffic

FuelEst.ee — Estonia fuel prices

Web (mobile-responsive). fuelest.ee

  • Crowdsourced fuel price comparison covering all Estonian chains (Circle K, Neste, Olerex, Alexela)
  • Shows lowest prices from last 12 hours

Pricing: Free

Notes: More accurate for rural Estonia than Waze. Not an app — bookmark the site

Waze — cities only

iOS, Android.

  • Crowdsourced fuel prices, speed camera warnings, construction alerts

Pricing: Free

Baltic fit: Works well near Tallinn/Riga/Vilnius. Rural data is stale or absent — few active users. Speed camera warnings are useful on highways but camera map apps (tarktee.ee, csdd.lv) are more reliable

Other tools

Polarsteps — trip documentation

iOS, Android.

  • Automatic background route tracking — only 4% battery for a full day
  • Photos geotagged to locations, creates shareable travel journal
  • Works offline, syncs when connected

Pricing: Free (basic)

Known issue: Occasional distance tracking inaccuracies. Still the best in its category

Fuelio — fuel tracking

Android (iOS: use Drivvo).

  • Fuel consumption tracking, cost logging per tank

Pricing: Free

Komoot — route discovery

iOS, Android, Web.

  • Recommended by Estonian riders on biker.ee. Use mountain bike profile to see surface types (asphalt, gravel, forest)
  • Not motorcycle-specific but good for planning gravel routes

Pricing: Free (limited). Regional maps ~9 EUR each or 30 EUR all

Phone mounting

The vibration problem is real. Apple officially warns motorcycle vibrations damage iPhone camera OIS/autofocus. Gravel roads amplify it significantly.

Mount Vibration reduction Best for
Quad Lock + Vibration Dampener >90% Adventure/gravel riding — preferred by ADV riders
SP Connect + Anti-Vibration Module ~60% Road touring, light adventure

Best option for gravel: Use a dedicated cheap/old phone for navigation on handlebars (connected to main phone's hotspot). Main phone stays in jacket pocket, camera intact. Multiple forum riders recommend this approach.

Cell coverage

Operators: Telia (best rural), Elisa, Tele2. Telia has 98.7% network availability — the pick for touring.

  • Cities and highways: Full 4G/5G coverage
  • Islands (Saaremaa, Hiiumaa): Coverage exists, no systematic dead zones
  • Soomaa, Haanja, deep forest/bogs: May drop to 2G/EDGE or lose signal briefly. Download offline maps before entering
  • EU roaming: Works throughout all three countries and Finland
